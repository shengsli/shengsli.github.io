2 evolution of hardware to parallelism

  • limitation:
    • Moore's law: transistors per processor
    • power wall: clock frequency cannot be increased without exceeding air cooling
    • memory wall: access to data is a limiting factor
    • instruction-level parallelism (ILP) wall
  • explicit parallel mechanism and explicit parallel programming are required for performance scaling.

3 software engineering consideration

  • problem: Amdahl's law: scaling will be limited by the serial fraction of your program.
  • solution: scale the parallel part of your program faster than the part using data parallelism.
  • problem: locking, access to memory, communication and overhead will strange scaling.
  • solution: use programming approaches with good data locality and low overhead and avoid locks.
  • problem: parallelism introduces new debugging challenges: deadlocks and race conditions.
  • solution: use structured programming strategies to avoid these by design, improving maintainability.

4 why structured programming with patterns is good?

  • patterns are best practices for solving specific problems
  • patterns can be used to organize your code, leading to algorithms that are more scalable and maintainable.
  • a pattern a particular "algorithmic structure" with an efficient implementation.
  • good parallel pattern with low-overhead implementations.
